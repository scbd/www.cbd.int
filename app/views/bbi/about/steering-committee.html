Bio-Bridge Initiative Steering Committee

Terms of Reference and Rules of Procedure

The governance and operations of the Bio-Bridge Initiative (BBI) will be overseen by a Steering Committee established for this purpose, under the overall strategic oversight by the Bureau of the Subsidiary Body on Implementation (SBI) on behalf of Conference of the Parties.

    Functions

  1. The Steering Committee will be the decision-making body responsible for providing management oversight to the implementation of BBI. Roles and responsibilities will include the following:

    • Provide strategic guidance and oversight of the BBI activities;
    • Review and approve priorities for the BBI medium-term work programmes;
    • Approve annual work plans proposed by the Secretariat;
    • Monitor implementation of the BBI activities and programmes and review progress reports prepared by the Secretariat;
    • Provide guidance on BBI operational policies and procedures, including project application procedures and selection criteria; and
    • Provide guidance on fundraising opportunities and activities.

    The Secretariat of the Convention on Biological Diversity will provide the necessary logistical and secretarial support to the work of the Steering Committee.

  2. The CBD Secretariat will provide support for the work of the Committee, including the necessary logistics and secretarial services.

    3. Composition

  3. The Steering Committee shall be composed of:

    • A government representative from each of the five regional groupings (Africa, Asia and the Pacific, Central and Eastern Europe, Latin America and the Caribbean and Western Europe and Others Group);
    • The Chair of the Subsidiary Body on Implementation or his/her designated representative from the Bureau; and
    • A representative each of the major donor countries, institutions and organizations providing substantial funding support to the Initiative.

  4. A senior representative of the CBD Executive Secretary will participate in an ex-officio capacity, and serve as Secretary to the Committee.
  5. Members of the Steering Committee will be selected by the CBD Executive Secretary in consultation with the SBI Bureau based on nominations made by Parties and relevant organizations. The selection will take into account the expertise and experience of the nominated officials with respect to technical and scientific cooperation under the Convention and its Protocols and will give due regard to balanced geographic and gender representation. Committee members will serve for a term of two years, with a possibility of renewal for one additional two-year term.
  6. The Committee may, as appropriate, invite resource persons to support its deliberations on BBI.
  7. In the interim, until the Steering Committee is established, a Project Review Panel was convened in mid-2016 to provide a transparent assessment of candidate BBI projects and will play the advisory role. The Project Review Panel will be discontinued once the Steering Committee is established.

    8. Chair

  8. The Chair of the Committee will be elected on a biennial rotational basis by members of the Committee as listed in paragraph 3 during each COP cycle.
  9. If the Chair is unable to attend a particular meeting or fulfil his/her roles over a period of time, the Committee will designate any other member to act as the Chair of that meeting or period of time.

    10. Meetings

  10. The Committee will meet face-to-face at least once a year and, as appropriate, continue to work intersessionally through other means as necessary (e.g. e-mail and/or teleconference). To the extent possible, the face-to-face meetings will be held in the margins of other relevant meetings. The costs for the participation of Steering Committee members representing developing country Parties and Parties with economies in transition will be covered by BBI.
  11. Members of the Steering Committee will be requested to confirm their attendance of the Committee meetings as early as possible and at least four weeks in advance.

    12. Agenda and Documentation

  12. In consultation with the Chair of the Committee, the CBD Secretariat will prepare the provisional agenda and supporting documentation for each meeting.
  13. The provisional agenda, together with supporting documents, for each meeting will be distributed by the CBD Secretariat to the members of the Committee at least six weeks before the opening of the meeting.
  14. The Secretariat will, in consultation with the Chair, include items proposed by a member of the Committee and received by the Secretariat after the provisional agenda has been produced, but before the opening of the meeting.
  15. The working language of the BBI Steering Committee will be English.

    16. Quorum

  16. At least 5 of the members of the Steering Committee referred to in paragraph 3 above must be present to constitute a quorum. The quorum shall be verified by the Chair at the time of the adoption of decisions by the Committee.

    17. Decision-making

  17. Decisions of the Steering Committee will be taken by consensus as far as possible.
